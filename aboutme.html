What We Do

01 Weekly Meetups Structured sessions covering algorithms, data structures, problem-solving techniques, and guest speaker talks. Weekly
02 Hackathons 48-hour intensive building events where teams prototype real solutions to real problems under real pressure. Semester
03 Competitive Programming Training camps and mock contests to prepare members for Codeforces, LeetCode, ICPC, and national olympiads. Ongoing
04 Research Projects Collaborative research on ML, data analysis, and systems — with opportunities to publish or present. Year-round
05 Industry Workshops Hands-on sessions with tools used in modern tech companies, led by industry professionals. Monthly

Our History

2020

Club Founded

Started by 8 CS students who wanted a place to practice competitive programming outside of class. First official meetup held in Room A-101.

2021

First Hackathon

InfoHack v1 — 32 participants, 8 teams, one weekend. The winning team built a real-time campus bus tracker still in use today.

2022

National Recognition

Two members placed in the top 10 at the National Informatics Olympiad. The club expanded to 6 active program tracks.

2023

Industry Partnerships

Signed collaboration agreements with 3 tech companies for workshops, internship referrals, and sponsored events.

2024–25

Growth & Research

Membership passed 100. Two research papers co-authored by members were submitted to international conferences.

2026

Now

120+ members across 6 tracks. InfoHack 2026 is our biggest event yet — and we're just getting started.
